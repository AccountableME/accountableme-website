The world holds you accountable for the big things.
But who's watching the rest?

Your boss tracks your KPIs. Your team knows if you shipped. Your family knows if you showed up. But the promise to yourself that you'd read more, exercise consistently, start that project, deepen your faith practice: nobody sees those goals. Nobody asks. And when you fall behind, the only person you've let down is you.

That's not a willpower problem. It's a structure problem. Research shows that people who share goals with someone they trust and get regular check-ins are dramatically more likely to follow through. When you know your partner will be notified if you don't act, that anticipation becomes the motivator. You already have the drive. You just need the system.

"It's amazing to me that we work to live up to our commitments to others but not ourselves." -- Ray Guzman, Co-Founder

Here's how it works

Four steps to actually doing the things you said you'd do.

01

Set your goals

Tell the AI what you want to accomplish in any area of life. It breaks big goals into daily and weekly actions with a plan tailored to you.

02

Invite your people

This is why it works. Invite friends, family, or your coach as accountability partners. Different goals call for different people. Your running buddy for fitness. Your mentor for career. Research shows people who commit to goals alongside peers are dramatically more likely to follow through.

03

Get nudged, not nagged

The app nudges you first, privately, giving you grace to act on your own. If no action is taken, your accountability partner is notified. That tension is what changes behavior. It's loss aversion meets social accountability.

04

Build your Integrity Score

Every follow-through builds your score (0-100). Every missed commitment costs you. It's your reputation with yourself, quantified, visible, and shared with the people walking with you.

Your Integrity Score

A simple way to see how well you're keeping your promises to yourself.

Most apps track what you did. AccountableME measures who you're becoming. Your Integrity Score (0-100) reflects how consistently you follow through on the commitments you've made to yourself.

It rises when you show up. It drops when you don't. Over time, it becomes the clearest picture of how consistently you're following through on the things that matter most to you.

Share it with your partners or keep it private. Hide it anytime if you'd rather focus on the doing than the measuring. But most people find that seeing the number changes their relationship with follow-through entirely.

It's not a game. It's a tool that helps you see, at a glance, whether you're becoming the person you're working to be.

Why it works

This isn't a theory. It's behavioral science.

3.5x

Public commitment multiplies follow-through

People who commit to goals within a peer group significantly outperform those who set goals alone. Making your intentions visible to someone you trust changes your relationship with the goal.

NBER Working Paper Series, 2012
95%

Buddy systems drive adherence

Participants paired with accountability partners achieved dramatically higher adherence rates than those incentivized individually. The relationship matters more than the reward.

UC Berkeley, 2024
Weekly

Optimal check-in cadence

Research consistently shows that weekly peer interactions hit the sweet spot: frequent enough to maintain urgency, spaced enough to avoid fatigue. Brief 10-15 minute check-ins outperform longer, less frequent sessions.

University of Virginia, 2015
76%

Structured accountability with a specific partner wins

People who shared weekly progress with a specific accountability partner achieved their goals 76% of the time. Compare that to 35% for those working alone.

Dr. Gail Matthews, Dominican University, 2015

AccountableME isn't guesswork. It's built on these research findings. Specific partners with weekly check-ins, mutual visibility, optimal nudge timing, and a measurable score that reflects your integrity. Every feature exists because the science says it works.

See it in action

60 seconds. No voiceover. Just the truth about accountability.

"Why not just text my friend?"

You can. And for about two weeks, they'll check in. Then life gets in the way for both of you. They forget to ask. You forget to update them. There's no escalation, no built-in tension that makes you act. Nobody wants to be the person who nags their friend, and nobody wants to be nagged.

Research shows people working alone hit their goals 35% of the time. Add structured accountability with a specific partner who checks in weekly? That jumps to 76%. Texting lacks structure, visibility, and the escalation mechanic that actually changes behavior.

AccountableME removes the awkwardness. The app nudges you first, privately. If no action is taken, your partner is notified. That tension is the real motivator. Your partner gets automatic visibility into your progress without having to ask. The AI fills the gaps when your partner is busy. Your Integrity Score gives you a real, quantifiable measure of whether you're keeping your word.

$12.99/month is less than one skipped gym visit. The question isn't whether you can afford it. It's what it's costing you to keep letting yourself down.

Why we built this

It was the Fourth of July. Ray pulled out his annual goals list and realized the big things were on track -- the board commitments, the company milestones, the family trip. The world held him accountable for those. But the personal goals? The ones only he knew about? They were slipping. Again.

He went looking for an app. Something that would pair him with someone who'd actually ask, "Did you do it?" He didn't find one. Every app was a solitary habit tracker that relied on the very thing he was trying to build: self-discipline.

If a serial founder with a military background can admit he needs help following through on his personal goals, then maybe it's okay for everyone else to say the same thing.

Ray and Nakisha have been married 30 years and live in Franklin, Tennessee with their five daughters. They built AccountableME together. Because that vulnerability isn't a marketing angle. It's the truth that invites everyone in.
